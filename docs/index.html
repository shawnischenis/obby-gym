Learning Roblox parkour

Obby Gym is a Roblox Studio simulator and Python learning stack for agents that run, steer, jump, land, and continue across unseen obstacle courses.

Phase 01 · Generate

Every layout is sampled from a seed and feasibility-checked before an agent ever sees it.

A small world for studying general control

Individual Roblox jumps are simple. Chaining them is not. The agent must preserve momentum, time takeoff, steer in the air, establish a landing, switch to the next target, and repeat. Small errors compound with course length.

We turn that problem into a reproducible benchmark: every course is generated from a seed, checked against a feasibility model, simulated with Roblox physics, and exposed through a synchronous structured-observation interface.

The Studio generator adds a scripted-oracle pass and uses Roblox’s RNG, so the same seed produces a different course there.

Curriculum

Trained in stages

The promoted policy is the product of a 23-stage curriculum — each skill learned in isolation, then recombined. A checkpoint advances only after clearing at least 90% combined completion on development and untouched validation seeds; failed runs stay in the experiment log.

Stage 15/5 deterministic, zero hazards

Locomotion

Run a 200-stud runway without falling off — promoted only after an actuator audit replaced twitchy key taps with held continuous movement.

Stage 2128/128 via privileged DAgger

First required jump

A 7-stud gap momentum alone cannot cross. The valid takeoff window was measured with a jump-pulse sweep before any policy trained on it.

Stages 3–593.8% combined

Narrow gaps

Fresh-seed DAgger repaired seed overfitting; the promoted checkpoint then generalized to Stage 5 with no additional training at all.

Stages 13–15Up to 98.4% combined

Gap boundaries

Wide and short gap ranges were learned in isolation, then rehearsed together with overlapping sampling to stop the two skills interfering.

Stages 7–8128/128 each

Landing heights

High-to-low and low-to-high landings transferred directly from the gap curriculum — no dedicated training run was needed.

Stages 9–1099.2%, then 95.3% at ±18°

Approach angles

Small negative angles failed asymmetrically at 52.9%. Steering-aware DAgger with a checkpoint-offset oracle corrected it.

Stage 11122/128 combined

Combined geometry

Gap, landing height, and approach angle vary together on every segment — the full single-jump distribution.

Stage 20122/128 · promoted

Chained mixed segments

Narrow beams and stairs join the distribution, and PPO fine-tunes with weighted replay of every earlier skill family.

Stages 21+In progress

Open problems

Four-segment chains and the required-jump correction are documented, in-progress work — not silently folded into the claims above.

Training recipe

Teach broadly, then recover from the student’s mistakes

  1. 1Train a privileged policy with full course geometry.
  2. 2Distill its actions into a limited-sensing student.
  3. 3Roll out the student and relabel its off-distribution states with DAgger.
  4. 4Fine-tune conservatively with PPO while replaying earlier curriculum stages.
