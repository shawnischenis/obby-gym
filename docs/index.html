01 / MOTIVATION

An obby is a compact test of general control.

Walking is easy. Reliable parkour is not: the policy must preserve momentum, time takeoff, steer in the air, land, recognize the next target, and repeat. Small per-obstacle errors compound rapidly across a course.

We procedurally vary gap, landing height, approach angle, beams, stairs, and obstacle count. Every course has a seed, deterministic manifest, validity oracle, and held-out evaluation split.

04 / ENGINEERING LESSONS

The simulator contract was part of the algorithm.

Fixed-duration actions

Refreshing held movement every physics tick replaced twitchy “key tapping” with continuous control.

Synchronous cohorts

Rebuilding one course while other lanes moved corrupted transition timing. Cohort resets restored valid rollouts.

Curriculum replay

Earlier skills remained in the sampling mixture so new geometry did not silently erase locomotion or jump timing.

Honest boundaries

Unreliable 9–10 stud jumps were moved to an optional stress test instead of stalling the core distribution.

Distribution-aware imitation

Cloning perfect trajectories was insufficient; learner-visited DAgger states were decisive.

Promotion, not anecdotes

Checkpoints require deterministic development and untouched validation partitions, plus regression suites.
