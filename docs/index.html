Procedural control in Roblox

Learning parkour

Obby Gym is a Roblox Studio simulator and Python learning stack for agents that run, steer, jump, land, and continue across unseen obstacle courses.

GitHub Method Experiment log

A small world for studying general control.

Individual Roblox jumps are simple. Chaining them is not. The agent must preserve momentum, time takeoff, steer in the air, establish a landing, switch to the next target, and repeat. Small errors compound with course length.

We turn that problem into a reproducible benchmark: every course is generated from a seed, checked against a feasibility model, simulated with Roblox physics, and exposed through a synchronous structured-observation interface.

Environment

Variation is the training data.

The curriculum starts with locomotion and one jump, then introduces distance, landing height, approach angle, beams, stairs, and multi-obstacle sequences.

Training recipe

Teach broadly, then recover from the student’s mistakes.

  1. 1Train a privileged policy with full course geometry.
  2. 2Distill its actions into a limited-sensing student.
  3. 3Roll out the student and relabel its off-distribution states with DAgger.
  4. 4Fine-tune conservatively with PPO while replaying earlier curriculum stages.

Evaluation

Landing once is not enough.

The promoted policy is tested on chained geometry where the second approach begins with the velocity and pose produced by the first landing.

What mattered

The environment contract was part of the algorithm.

01

Hold actions for a fixed duration.

Refreshing movement through each physics interval replaced twitchy key tapping with continuous control.

02

Reset cohorts synchronously.

Rebuilding one course while other lanes moved corrupted timing; synchronized resets restored valid transitions.

03

Train on recovery states.

Post-landing starts and learner-visited DAgger states exposed the distribution that perfect first approaches missed.

04

Keep honest task boundaries.

Unreliable extreme jumps remain stress tests instead of silently entering the claimed solved distribution.
